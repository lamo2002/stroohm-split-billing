Split Billing Models

Four approaches to cost allocation between company and employee

Price Threshold

Company pays up to €X/kWh. Employee pays excess per kWh.

Monthly Budget

Company pays up to €X/month. Employee pays excess.

Day/Time

Company pays during work hours only.

Percentage

Company pays X%, employee pays Y%.

Objective: Establish fleet charging cost control without restricting charger access or increasing administrative burden. Introduce shared cost responsibility to encourage cost-conscious charging behavior.

Price Threshold Model

Per-kWh cost allocation with inherent limitations

How it works

Company pays for charging up to a price limit (e.g. €0.40/kWh). If the charger costs more, the employee pays the difference per kWh.

Strengths for Operators

  • ✓Deters expensive chargers — drivers avoid high prices
  • ✓Direct price control — sets max €/kWh you pay
  • ✓Familiar model — common in union agreements

Strengths for Drivers

  • ✓Clear threshold — know exactly what's covered
  • ✓No monthly cap — unlimited at/below threshold
  • ✓Fair split — only pay for premium choice

Our concerns

  • ✗Total costs unpredictable — 1000 kWh at €0.39 = €390
  • ✗Only covers kWh — parking, idle fees need separate rules
  • ✗Dynamic pricing confusion — price changes mid-session
  • ✗Hard to explain — "€0.40/kWh but parking separate..."
  • ✗Operational burden — more driver and finance questions

Monthly Budget

Provides fixed cost ceiling for financial planning

How it works

Company sets a monthly allowance per driver. Company pays all charging up to that limit. Employee pays any excess automatically.

Strengths for Operators

  • •Costs predictable — fixed maximum per driver per month
  • •Covers ALL fees — kWh, parking, idle, starting
  • •Finance-friendly — monthly budget = max cost
  • •Simple to communicate — "Your monthly charging budget"

Strengths for Drivers

  • •Full transparency — see remaining budget anytime
  • •No surprises — see cost split before charging
  • •Self-regulation — select cost-effective chargers
  • •Fair experience — company covers normal use

Requirements

  • •Budget sizing — depends on fleet and usage patterns
  • •High-mileage risk — some may exhaust budget quickly
  • •No explicit price cap — Price Threshold if needed

Key insight

Drivers organically gravitate toward cost-efficient chargers. Higher-priced options consume allowance faster, creating threshold-like behavior through incentive alignment.

Our recommendation: Monthly Budget gives you cost control now. Threshold follows if needed.

Our Recommendation

Delivering on cost control for fleets

Start with Monthly Budget

Defined cost ceiling. A fixed monthly allowance per driver provides finance teams with concrete figures for budget planning rather than variable estimates.

Behavioral impact. When personal funds are involved beyond the cap, drivers naturally gravitate toward more cost-effective charging options.

Straightforward implementation. A single monthly allowance is easy to communicate. Price Threshold requires explaining multiple fee types and dynamic pricing.

The sequencing

1

Deploy Monthly Budget for cost predictability

2

Collect usage data to inform enhancements

3

Add Price Threshold when data shows it's needed

Price Threshold remains available. Shared cost responsibility typically achieves natural price optimization with less complexity.
